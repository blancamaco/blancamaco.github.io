Improving the Search Engine of Tuna

Challenges

The existing search feature on Tuna lacked the necessary refinement options, resulting in users struggling to find specific sounds. They had to write exactly what they wanted. The lack of filters, search by multiple tags, and better sorting options made it difficult for users to discover sounds that matched their preferences. This led to a decrease in sound discovery and overall user satisfaction, receiving user feedback from different channels regarding the difficulties in finding the sounds that fit their needs.

Research and Discovery

During the research phase, we first asked users in our community to gain valuable insights to understand their search behaviors, frustrations, and expectations regarding the sound search. We asked about what they were interested to search, if it was sounds, soundboards, users, tags…

Key insights gained from the research phase included:

  • Users expressed frustration with the lack of filtering options, as they were unable to narrow down their search results effectively.
  • Many users desired a more intuitive and user-friendly search interface that would make it easier to navigate and interact with the search feature.
  • The ability to discover sounds based on specific themes or tags was highly desired by users, as it would enhance their overall experience.
  • For them, it was important to search also users Soundboards.

Ideation and Conceptualization

During the ideation and conceptualization phase, we engaged in brainstorming sessions to generate ideas and explore potential search solutions, improvements, and layouts. We encouraged open and collaborative discussions with the entire team (Developers and Product Manager), allowing everyone to contribute their thoughts and suggestions.

Validating with user tests

To ensure that we were making the right design decision with the multi-content search, we reached a point where we had two distinct alternatives for the search layout. In order to gather user opinions and feedback, we conducted an unmoderated user test. This test allowed us to understand how users interacted with the different alternatives and helped us make an informed decision on the final design.

Flow 1 - Traditional search input

The user sees a page with all the sounds found in the search. He can then filter based on type of content; Sounds, Soundboards or Users.

Flow 2 - Mega-dropdown search

The user sees a full screen modal with all the content types based on the search.

Results highlights
  • Users are mainly interested in searching sounds, but a considerable amount, 85%, also would like to search Soundboards.
  • Usually prefer to search for a specific type of content.
  • Flow 2 - Too crowded dropdown, they felt overwhelmed, they only want to see the type of content they are looking for.
    • “If you're searching for something, you know you're searching for either a sound, a soundboard, or a user. It's not ambiguous.”

Solution

To address these challenges, we implemented a series of improvements to the search engine of Tuna.

We introduced a user-friendly and intuitive user interface (UI) that allowed users to easily navigate and interact with the search feature, showing suggestions on the search field.

We added a second-level search by Sounds, Soundboards or Users.

On a third level, we introduced various filters such as tags with relevant keywords and categories. These filters enabled users to narrow down their search results based on specific criteria, making it easier to find the desired sounds.

We also added new sorting options based on popularity and more reproduced sounds. This flexibility allowed users to prioritize their search results based on their preferences.

We promoted the categories discovery, adding an interactive selector to easily navigate through them, and redesigning the categories pages.

Results

The introduction of these improvements to the search engine of Tuna had a significant impact on sound discovery. The refined search experience led to a 28% increase in sound discovery, impacting the number of users who after a search ended up using a sound, as users were able to find sounds more precisely and efficiently.

Conclusion

By focusing on refining the search engine of Tuna and providing users with better UX and UI, we were able to greatly enhance the sound discovery experience. The addition of filters, tags, and sorting options allowed users to find sounds that matched their preferences, leading to increased user satisfaction and engagement with the platform.
